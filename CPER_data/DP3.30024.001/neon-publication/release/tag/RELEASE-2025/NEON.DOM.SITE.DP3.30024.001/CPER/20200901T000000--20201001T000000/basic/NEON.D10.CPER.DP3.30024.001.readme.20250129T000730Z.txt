DATA PRODUCT INFORMATION

------------------------



ID: NEON.DOM.SITE.DP3.30024.001



Name: Elevation - LiDAR



Description: All features and bare earth elevation given in meters in the NAVD88 (Geoid12A realization) reference frame as a DSM and DTM respectively. Bare earth is created by classifying and removing vegetation and man-made structures. Both the DSM and DTM are mosaicked onto a spatially uniform grid at 1 m spatial resolution in 1 km by 1 km tiles.



NEON Science Team Supplier: Airborne Observation Platform



Abstract: The elevation product, in the form of a DTM, provides information on terrain structure, and is an important data layer in spatially driven models of landscape processes. Currently, LIDAR sensors provide the most efficient means for collecting an accurate and dense sample of the terrain among competing remote sensing or positioning systems. For example, high-resolution digital stereo photogrammetry can compete in terms of point density in open terrain, but suffers from sparse sampling beneath tree canopy.  The DSM provides information on the structure of surface features, including derived vegetation structure, which can be used as a proxy to estimate important ecological quantities of interest.

Latency:
AOP data will be available 60 days after the final collection day at a site.



Brief Design Description: The elevation products supplied include a digital terrain model (DTM) and a digital surface model (DSM). The DTM includes only elevations that relate to the physical terrain surface, while the DSM also includes elevations that relate to surface features (eg. buildings, vegetation). Elevation values for both the DTM and DSM are reported with reference to NAVD88 (Geoid12A realization), while horizontal coordinates are referenced to the ITRF00 datum, projected to the Universal Transverse Mercator (UTM) mapping frame. The DTM and DSM are distributed as 1 km by 1 km tiles, which have corners spatially referenced to an even kilometer. The product is stored in GeoTIFF format.



Brief Study Area Description: NEON AOP data are planned for yearly collects at all NEON sites at 90% of maximum greenness or greater. Coverage is planned to include at least 95% of NIS Tower Airshed area as well as at least 80% of a minimum 10km x 10 km box around that. All acquisitions are subject to change due to weather conditions as well as program planning changes.



Sensor(s): NEON Airborne Observation Platform (AOP) LiDAR - Optech Gemini



Keywords: aerial, digital terrain model (DTM), remote sensing, digital surface model (DSM), 3D, light detection and ranging (LiDAR), digital elevation model (DEM), bare earth, elevation, airborne

-----------------------

NEON data files are named using a series of component abbreviations separated by periods. File naming conventions

DATA QUALITY AND VERSIONING
---------------------------

NEON data are initially published with a status of Provisional, in which updates to data and/or processing
algorithms will occur on an as-needed basis, and query reproducibility cannot be guaranteed. Once data are published
as part of a Data Release, they are no longer provisional, and are associated with a stable DOI.

To learn more about provisional versus released data, please visit
https://www.neonscience.org/data-revisions-releases.
